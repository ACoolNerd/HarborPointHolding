# Connecticut Public-Records and Foreclosure Research Gate

This document is an operational issue-spotting checklist, not legal advice.

## Current Connecticut framework

Connecticut mortgage foreclosure is a court process. Owner-occupied one-to-four-family borrowers may have access to the Judicial Branch Foreclosure Mediation Program and must follow applicable court deadlines. Harbor Point and KassConnect must never obstruct or replace communication with a lender, servicer, attorney, court, or housing counselor.

Municipal property-tax collection is handled locally by municipal tax collectors. Recorded municipal liens, tax-sale notices, assessment records, land records, and court filings may create research signals, but every record must be verified against its official source and date.

## Approved research purposes

- Identify parcels and owners for legitimate real-estate business development
- Build aggregated market and redevelopment reports
- Identify publicly announced municipal redevelopment priorities
- Offer a voluntary real-estate options consultation
- Connect owners with appropriately licensed professionals

## Prohibited uses

- Harassment or repeated unwanted contact
- Automated texting/calling without sufficient consent
- False affiliation with a bank, court, municipality, government, or counselor
- Publicly exposing an owner’s alleged hardship
- Predicting protected characteristics or personal vulnerability
- Calling a filing a foreclosure judgment when it is only a complaint, notice, lien, or lis pendens
- Legal, debt-relief, foreclosure-rescue, bankruptcy, tax, or financial advice
- Promising that a sale will stop foreclosure or cure a default

## Verification checklist

Before any record becomes a lead:

- [ ] Official source URL and access date recorded
- [ ] Parcel/address matched across at least two reliable sources
- [ ] Owner name verified from current assessor or land record
- [ ] Filing type and procedural status correctly classified
- [ ] Owner-occupied residential vs. commercial/investment identified
- [ ] Existing counsel or represented-party concerns reviewed
- [ ] Contact source is lawful and appropriate
- [ ] Outreach script is approved for the lead category
- [ ] Do-not-contact and suppression database checked
- [ ] Human reviewer approves release

## Filing-status taxonomy

Use neutral statuses:

- Public tax delinquency notice
- Recorded municipal lien
- Foreclosure complaint filed
- Lis pendens recorded
- Mediation indicated
- Judgment entered
- Sale date scheduled
- Law day scheduled
- Case withdrawn/dismissed
- Title transferred
- Status requires attorney verification

Never collapse these into a single “foreclosure list.”

## Outreach standard

Use one respectful letter or manually initiated contact based on verified public business information. State how the contact information was obtained when appropriate. Provide direct identification, brokerage/entity role, no-obligation wording, and an opt-out.

Suggested opening:

> I am reaching out as a Connecticut real estate professional because publicly available property records indicate there may be a property decision to evaluate. I am not affiliated with your lender, the court, or any government agency, and I am not offering legal or debt-relief advice. My role is limited to discussing voluntary real-estate options if that would be useful.

## Required referrals

Maintain a reviewed referral list for:

- Connecticut foreclosure-defense attorneys
- HUD-approved housing counselors
- Probate and estate attorneys
- Bankruptcy attorneys
- CPAs and tax professionals
- Licensed appraisers
- Environmental professionals
- Architects and engineers
- Commercial lenders
- Insurance professionals

## Data retention

- Store only fields needed for the business purpose
- Keep source and verification timestamps
- Restrict access by role
- Encrypt sensitive operational data
- Delete or suppress stale and inaccurate records
- Honor opt-outs immediately
- Do not publish lead-level records to a public GitHub repository
