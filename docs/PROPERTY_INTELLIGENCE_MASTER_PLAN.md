# Harbor Point / KassConnect Property Intelligence Master Plan

## Purpose

Build a compliant Greater Hartford lead system for Kassandra Gonzales that identifies public, permissioned, and referral-based property opportunities and routes them into the correct service lane.

## Service lanes

1. **Licensed brokerage** — listing, buyer representation, leasing, negotiation, transaction guidance, and brokerage activities through Keller Williams Realty / KW Legacy Partners where applicable.
2. **KGrace / KassConnect consultancy** — site-fit organization, property-readiness review, redevelopment research, vendor coordination, timeline mapping, and introductions to licensed professionals.
3. **Harbor Point opportunity intake** — owners may request a review of listing, as-is, investor, commercial, multifamily, reuse, or referral options.

Never imply that all three lanes are the same entity, that Kassandra is acting as principal buyer when she is not, or that consultancy replaces legal, tax, engineering, appraisal, lending, insurance, environmental, permitting, or construction advice.

## Primary geography

Start with West Hartford, Hartford, East Hartford, Newington, Wethersfield, Farmington, New Britain, Bloomfield, Windsor, Manchester, Rocky Hill, and Glastonbury.

## Commercial opportunity categories

- Vacant retail and office
- Greyfields and obsolete shopping centers
- Brownfields requiring environmental professionals
- Underused industrial property
- Mixed-use and small multifamily 5+
- Tenant-occupied investment property
- Expiring leases and high vacancy
- Estate and partnership dispositions
- Municipal redevelopment targets
- Tax-delinquent property where information is lawfully public
- Judicial foreclosure filings where outreach is attorney-reviewed
- Properties with zoning, reuse, or adaptive-reuse potential
- Owner-occupied businesses considering sale-leaseback
- Nonprofit, school, religious, hospitality, medical-office, and special-purpose reuse

## Lead-source tiers

### Tier A — safest and highest priority

- Google Search and Business Profile inquiries
- Website forms and direct calls
- Past clients and sphere referrals
- Attorneys, CPAs, lenders, property managers, contractors, architects, engineers, environmental consultants, and insurance professionals
- Local chambers and economic-development organizations
- Municipal planning agendas, redevelopment plans, RFPs, and publicly posted opportunity lists
- Owner opt-ins from events, reports, newsletters, and valuation requests

### Tier B — public-record research with compliance controls

- Municipal assessor and GIS records
- Land records and recorded deeds/liens
- Tax collector delinquency and tax-sale notices
- Judicial foreclosure dockets and lis pendens filings
- Probate and estate filings where public
- Building, zoning, fire, health, demolition, and code-enforcement records where public
- Brownfield and greyfield inventories
- Planning and zoning applications
- Business registrations and UCC records where relevant

Public availability does not automatically authorize bulk scraping, resale, harassment, automated texting, or sensitive profiling. Each source must pass its terms-of-use, privacy, licensing, and outreach review.

### Tier C — licensed or contracted data

- CoStar, Crexi, LoopNet, Reonomy, ProspectNow, PropertyShark, ATTOM, CoreLogic, DataTree, MLS, and title-data products

Use only under a valid subscription and permitted export/API rights. Do not bypass access controls or copy protected listing content.

## Property scoring model

Score 0–100 using property facts, not personal vulnerability.

- Market fit: 20
- Asset / service-lane fit: 15
- Publicly documented vacancy or underuse: 15
- Redevelopment or reuse potential: 15
- Ownership / contact confidence: 10
- Timing signal: 10
- Estimated transaction value: 10
- Compliance confidence: 5

Automatic suppression:

- Do-not-contact request
- Unverified owner identity
- Protected or sensitive inference
- Active legal representation where direct contact would be inappropriate
- Source prohibits outreach or reuse
- Minor, protected person, or personal safety concern

## Repurposing opportunity workflow

1. Identify asset from a public or permissioned source.
2. Verify address, owner, parcel, use, zoning, and source date.
3. Record why the property may be underused without making unsupported claims.
4. Map possible reuse concepts as hypotheses only.
5. Flag professionals needed: zoning counsel, architect, engineer, environmental professional, lender, contractor, appraiser, insurance, property manager.
6. Create a one-page opportunity brief.
7. Route to Kassandra for human approval.
8. Conduct respectful, one-to-one outreach with source attribution and opt-out.
9. Log outcome and suppress future contact when requested.

## Foreclosure and delinquency lane

The system may monitor public filings, but it must not market itself as foreclosure rescue, legal assistance, debt relief, or guaranteed purchase.

Safe offer:

> A confidential real estate options consultation to discuss possible listing, sale, referral, or property-strategy paths. Homeowners should also consult their attorney, housing counselor, lender/servicer, and other qualified professionals.

Required controls:

- Attorney review of scripts and cadence
- No pressure, threats, countdowns, or false urgency
- No implication of affiliation with a court, bank, municipality, or government program
- No automated calls/texts without legally sufficient consent
- Immediate opt-out and suppression
- Separate owner-occupied residential rules from commercial or investment property
