Attribution Rule

Every ad, letter, and post links to its lane page — never the hub. The hub is for organic search, referrals, and the curious. Source tags do the rest.

Content Cadence

Publish source-backed market insights and substantiated proof only. Never use unverified results, testimonials, credentials, or transaction claims.

The Property Intelligence Engine

Combine permitted public records, authorized licensed data, source dates, verification status, and human review. Research creates a review queue; it does not authorize contact.

Layer III

The Connection — How a Lead Flows

1 · Visitor lands on a lane page (source known instantly)

Ad, letter, or search brings them to their lane. The page's SOURCE_TAG is already set — attribution starts before they type a word.

↓
2 · Conversational intake qualifies them (60 seconds)

Chat-style quiz captures property type, situation, timeline, town, and contact info. Feels like a conversation, works like a qualification form.

↓
3 · Submission is verified

The page submits the intake and source tag to HubSpot. The visitor sees success only after HubSpot confirms receipt. A Deal and routing task are created separately by the approved backend or supported automation.

↓
4 · The neutral lead queue receives the alert

The alert identifies source lane, town, and timeline without implying an individual owner or operator. New matters default to harborpoint_market.

↓
5 · Approved consultation is offered

A booking button appears only after a neutral Harbor Point event URL is approved. Attribution remains attached to the booking.

↓
6 · Governed follow-up covers the gap

Supported automations may create tasks or approved messages. Full HubSpot workflows require a qualifying subscription; referrals and external outreach remain subject to consent and human approval.

Layer IV

The CRM — HubSpot Setup

Custom Properties (create these first)

  • lead_source_page — which lane page (the tag)
  • property_type — asset type from intake
  • goal — situation / motivation
  • timeline — urgency band
  • city — CT town (standard field)
  • deal_lane — Sell Fast / Lifeline / Commercial / Advisory / Landlord

Pipeline Stages

  • New Lead — just submitted, untouched
  • Contacted — approved contact occurred or the lead booked
  • Consultation Held — call/meeting done
  • Offer / Proposal Out — number or plan delivered
  • Under Contract / Engaged — signed
  • Closed Won / Closed Lost — with lost-reason logged

Workflows (automations)

  • Instant Alert: successful form submit → neutral lead-queue alert with lane, town, and timeline
  • Priority Review: time-sensitive inquiry → human review; no pressure or vulnerability-based targeting
  • No-Booking Task: create approved follow-up tasks or messages, stop on booking, reply, opt-out, or suppression
  • Stale Lead Sweep: 14 days no activity → task on the operations dashboard to review, nurture, or close

Views & Reporting

  • Today Board: New + Hot leads, sorted by timeline
  • Lane Report: leads & closes by lead_source_page — shows which marketing actually pays
  • Town Heatmap: leads by city — informs market research without protected-class targeting
  • Weekly digest email: auto-sent Monday 8 AM
Layer V

How the Market Desk Operates

8:00 AM — Review the neutral lead queue

Validate new submissions, source tags, delivery status, consent, suppression, and duplicate records before qualification.

8:15 AM — Apply the review gate

Time-sensitive or foreclosure-related matters are handled confidentially and routed for attorney or approved professional review where appropriate. Intake details are not used as pressure leverage.

10:30 AM — Run approved consultations

Record notes, disclosures, consent, next action, and associations on the HubSpot Deal. A booking does not itself create representation or a referral.

1:00 PM — Source and verify

Review permitted Connecticut and municipal sources, preserve provenance, resolve conflicts, and create research candidates. No outreach occurs until source rights, suppression, purpose, and approval are confirmed.

4:00 PM — Govern follow-up

Approved tasks and messages are monitored by SLA. Opt-outs, do-not-contact flags, consent limits, and failed deliveries stop the sequence.

Friday — Evidence informs the market plan

Review attribution, qualified opportunities, consultations, routing, suppression, data quality, and cost per approved opportunity before changing spend.
