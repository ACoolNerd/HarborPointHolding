Each lane is its own campaign with its own audience, its own message, and its own landing page. Traffic never hits a generic homepage — it lands on the page built for exactly that person.

Attribution Rule

Every ad, letter, and post links to its lane page — never the hub. The hub is for organic search, referrals, and the curious. Source tags do the rest.

Content Cadence

2 posts/week per active channel: one market insight, one proof point (closed deal, before/after, testimonial). Batch monthly.

The Sourcing Engine

Weekly pull of lis pendens filings + absentee-owner records from town land records → mail list → Lifeline & Landlord lanes. This is the proprietary edge.

Layer III

The Connection — How a Lead Flows

1 · Visitor lands on a lane page (source known instantly)

Ad, letter, or search brings them to their lane. The page's SOURCE_TAG is already set — attribution starts before they type a word.

↓
2 · Conversational intake qualifies them (60 seconds)

Chat-style quiz captures property type, situation, timeline, town, and contact info. Feels like a conversation, works like a qualification form.

↓
3 · One submission, three simultaneous actions

On completion the page fires the HubSpot Forms API with every answer + the source tag. HubSpot then: (a) creates/updates the contact, (b) drops them into the pipeline, (c) triggers her instant notification.

↓
4 · She gets the alert (with the source in the subject)

Email + mobile push: "New Lead — Lifeline — Waterbury — ASAP." She knows the lane, the town, and the urgency before she opens it.

↓
5 · Lead books direct via Calendly

The completion screen offers "Book Direct" with utm_source attached. Booking auto-syncs to her calendar and logs on the HubSpot contact.

↓
6 · Automated follow-up covers the gap

If no booking in 1 hour: HubSpot workflow sends a warm follow-up email with her Calendly link. Day 2 and Day 5 touches follow automatically until they book or reply.

Layer IV

The CRM — HubSpot Setup

Custom Properties (create these first)

  • lead_source_page — which lane page (the tag)
  • property_type — asset type from intake
  • goal — situation / motivation
  • timeline — urgency band
  • city — CT town (standard field)
  • deal_lane — Sell Fast / Lifeline / Commercial / Advisory / Landlord

Pipeline Stages

  • New Lead — just submitted, untouched
  • Contacted — she's reached out or they booked
  • Consultation Held — call/meeting done
  • Offer / Proposal Out — number or plan delivered
  • Under Contract / Engaged — signed
  • Closed Won / Closed Lost — with lost-reason logged

Workflows (automations)

  • Instant Alert: form submit → email + push to her with lane, town, timeline in subject
  • Hot Lead Flag: timeline = "ASAP" OR lane = "Lifeline" → mark priority, SMS-level alert
  • No-Booking Nudge: +1hr, +2d, +5d follow-up emails with Calendly link, stop on booking/reply
  • Stale Lead Sweep: 14 days no activity → task on her dashboard to call or close out

Views & Reporting

  • Today Board: New + Hot leads, sorted by timeline
  • Lane Report: leads & closes by lead_source_page — shows which marketing actually pays
  • Town Heatmap: leads by city — tells her where to aim the next mail drop
  • Weekly digest email: auto-sent Monday 8 AM
Layer V

How It Works For Her — A Tuesday

8:00 AM — One dashboard, coffee in hand

Opens the HubSpot Today Board. Three new leads overnight: a Lifeline in Waterbury (ASAP — already flagged hot), a Landlord in Bristol, an Advisory in Norwalk. Two already booked themselves on Calendly.

8:15 AM — Priority call

She calls the Waterbury Lifeline lead first. Every intake answer is on the contact record — she opens with "I saw you're a few payments behind and want to protect your equity" instead of "so tell me about your situation."

10:30 AM — Booked consultations run themselves

Calendly consults fire with Zoom/phone links already sent. Notes go straight onto the HubSpot record; she drags the deal card to "Consultation Held" when done.

1:00 PM — Sourcing hour (weekly)

Pulls new lis pendens filings from target town land records into the tracking sheet. Mail merge letters go out; those owners will land on the Lifeline page with its own source tag — closing the loop.

4:00 PM — Zero leads chased manually

Everyone who didn't book is inside the automated nudge sequence. Nothing sits untouched; nothing depends on her memory.

Friday — The numbers decide the marketing

Lane Report shows Lifeline mail converting at 3× the Facebook spend. Next month's budget shifts accordingly — the source tags turn every dollar into a measurable bet.
